An island in the southwestern part of the Republic of Korea. The jurisdiction of Jeju Special Self-Governing Province, a metropolitan autonomous body in the administrative region. It is the largest and most populous island of South Korea, with an area of 1,833.2 square kilometers and a population of about 660,000 people. It is a volcanic island formed by volcanic activity. For this reason, there are 370 parasitic volcanoes (Oreum) in the center of the island, which are 200m to 300m, and Hanon is one of them. Once upon a time there was a country called Tamna. Therefore, it was also called Tamdado because it is a name that refers to Jeju. It is also nicknamed the citrus soup.
